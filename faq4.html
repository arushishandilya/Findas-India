Will writing is the process of legally documenting your wishes for the distribution of your assets after your death.

It ensures that your property, savings, and personal belongings are passed on according to your instructions.

It helps prevent disputes among family members and ensures your estate is managed according to your wishes.

Without a will, the law decides how your assets are distributed, which may not align with your intentions.

Details of beneficiaries, a list of assets, the executor of your will, and any special instructions.

You can also include guardianship arrangements for minor children and charitable donations.

Any person over 18 years old and of sound mind can legally make a will.

The person must voluntarily decide and not be influenced or pressured by others.

Yes, it is important to update your will when major life changes occur.

Events such as marriage, divorce, the birth of a child, or acquiring significant assets may require revisions.

While it’s possible to write your own will, legal advice ensures it is valid and enforceable.

A lawyer can help avoid mistakes that might make your will unclear or invalid.

Yes, you can include online accounts, cryptocurrencies, and other digital property in your will.
